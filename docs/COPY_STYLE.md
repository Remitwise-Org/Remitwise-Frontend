# Copy Style Guide

Voice and tone conventions for product copy across all Remitwise surfaces.

## Audience

We write for **remittance senders and family financial managers** — people sending money home, managing household bills, and saving for goals. Assume they are busy, on mobile, and may not speak English as a first language.

## Principles

### 1. Clear over clever
Users are trying to complete a task. Don't make them decode marketing language.
- **Do:** "Your transfer is on its way."
- **Don't:** "Your remittance journey has commenced."

### 2. Specific over vague
Always include the number, currency, or timeframe.
- **Do:** "1,000 XLM sent to Maria's wallet."
- **Don't:** "Transaction successful."

### 3. Helpful over blaming
Errors happen. Focus on what to do next, not whose fault it is.
- **Do:** "Insufficient balance. Add funds to continue."
- **Don't:** "You don't have enough money."

## States

### Success
Confirm what happened and what's next.
- "Payment sent. It should arrive within 5 minutes."
- "Goal reached! You've saved 5,000 XLM for School Fees."

### Error
State the problem and the action to fix it.
- "Network error. Check your connection and try again."
- "This invoice is already paid. View your payment history."

### Empty
Explain what belongs here and how to get started.
- "No bills yet. Add your first bill to track payments."
- "Your savings goals will appear here. Create one to get started."

### Loading
Keep it short. Use consistent labels.
- "Loading your dashboard..."
- "Fetching rates..."

## Formatting

- Use sentence case for headings, buttons, and labels
- Use the local currency symbol where available
- Truncate long addresses: GABC...XYZ123
- Numbers over 1,000 use commas
- Dates use locale-aware format

## Button Labels

- "Send Payment"
- "Create Goal"
- "Add Bill"
- "View History"

Avoid: "Submit", "OK", "Click Here"

## Error Messages

Every error message must answer:
1. What happened?
2. What should the user do?
